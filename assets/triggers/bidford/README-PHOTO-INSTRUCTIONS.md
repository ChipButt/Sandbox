PHOTO INSTRUCTIONS FOR BIDFORD TRAIL

Take clear landscape or portrait photos of the specific real-world objects below. Save each photo using the exact filename listed.

General rules:
- Take the photo in daylight.
- Fill most of the frame with the object/sign/plaque.
- Avoid extreme angles.
- Avoid people, cars, dogs, or moving objects in the shot.
- Take the photo from the same public place where players will stand.
- Keep the object clearly readable.
- Do not crop too tightly.
- If possible, take 3-5 extra backup photos for later testing, but only one final reference photo should use the exact filename.
- Upload the final chosen files into /assets/triggers/bidford/

Required files:

1. trigger-01-bidford-bridge-info-board.jpg
Photo needed:
A clear photo of the Bidford Bridge information board, plaque, or the most distinctive public bridge feature that players can safely stand near and scan.

2. trigger-02-st-laurence-church-sign.jpg
Photo needed:
A clear photo of the St Laurence Church sign, noticeboard, entrance sign, or another stable public identifying feature outside the church.

3. trigger-03-war-memorial-plaque.jpg
Photo needed:
A clear photo of the main public-facing plaque or readable section of the Bidford-on-Avon war memorial.

4. trigger-04-big-meadow-sign.jpg
Photo needed:
A clear photo of the Big Meadow sign, entrance board, or another stable public sign/feature marking the meadow/riverside park.

5. trigger-05-riverside-feature.jpg
Photo needed:
A clear photo of a stable riverside feature near the public walking area, ideally something distinctive and unlikely to move, such as a sign, fixed marker, railing detail, information board, or permanent feature.

6. trigger-06-high-street-detail.jpg
Photo needed:
A clear photo of a stable public feature in central High Street, ideally a plaque, building sign, stone detail, or fixed sign that is publicly visible and unlikely to change.

After uploading these files:
- Upload the full app to GitHub Pages.
- Open the app on mobile.
- Enable dev mode by tapping the logo 7 times.
- Test each location.
- Use dev mode to check GPS and image match confidence.
- Adjust GPS coordinates and match threshold in /data/trails.js if needed.
